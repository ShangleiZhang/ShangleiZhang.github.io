CIRTL@IUPUI Web Design

  • Sept,2016
  • Website Design

Project Info

The Center for the Integration of Research, Teaching, and Learning (CIRTL) is a network of 43 universities in the USA and Canada working towards the goal of advancing undergraduate education through the professional development of future faculty. IUPUI and Indiana University, Bloomington are new members of the CIRTL network.

Working as a CIRTL teaching assistant, I take responsibilities of designing and programming the official website of CIRTL @ IUPUI. The goal is to design and build a website that provides trustworthy resources of CIRTL Programs for IUPUI students and faculties.

What I did

  • Front-End Development
  • Wireframes & Low- Fidelity prototypes
  • Logo Design
  • Responsive Design
  • High-Fidelity design & UI guidelines

Empathy for Real User

CIRTL Program aims to advance the teaching of STEM disciplines in higher education, and the target users of the website will be graduate students, postdocs, and faculties in STEM and other disciplines.

To understand their needs and expectations, I interviewed the CIRTL program leaders, coordinators, IUPUI students and faculties. The qualitative data gathered from the interviews and brainstorms provide insights to set up the design goals:

  • Clear Information Hierarchy
  • Design Consistency with IUPUI Web Design Pattern
  • Minimalism Design Solution
  • Responsive UI Crossing Multiple Platforms
  • Aesthetically Appealing & Visually Attractive

Competitive Websites Research


The CIRTL learning community consists of 43 universities across North America. Most of them created their local CIRTL websites to promote/share their learning programs.

After reviewing almost all the CIRTL local websites created by other universities, I specified the basic content and framework as design references.

Here are three examples from Cornell University, University of Pittsburgh and Boston University.


CIRTL at Cornell University website is well-maintained and a resourceful platform for other CIRTL participants to explore. However, the website design is more text-based than image based, which made the site less attractive for people to read through.


CIRTL at University of Pittsburgh website includes more than 20 pages, and they are organized in a clear hierarchy. Contents presented in single pages are grouped into several categories, which makes them easy to find.


CIRTL at Boston University doesn't own a dedicated website but a single-page site with limited information. Most contents/information for CIRTL are provided via external links and are distributed in various context. Those makes information retrieval extremely difficult.
